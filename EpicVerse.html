About the Musical NEW

70%

THE EPIC MUSICAL tells the legendary journey of Odysseus as he fights to return home after the Trojan War. Guided by hope and haunted by loss, Odysseus leads his loyal crew across dangerous seas filled with monsters, gods, and impossible trials.

The story unfolds through powerful, emotional songs that highlight each stage of their voyage—from moments of love and sacrifice to battles with mythical creatures like Scylla.

It's a powerful retelling of Odysseus’s journey home after the Trojan War, told through epic songs and dramatic scenes. The story begins with the war’s end, where Odysseus faces difficult choices, including tragic acts required by fate. He then sails home with his men, encountering dangers like the Lotus Eaters, the Cyclops Polyphemus, the enchantress Circe, and magical trials in the underworld. Along the way, the gods intervene—sometimes helping, sometimes hindering him—and he struggles with loss, pride, and temptation. After years of peril, Odysseus finally returns to Ithaca, where suitors have overrun his home. Using his wits and strength, he defeats them and reunites with his wife Penelope, though the journey has left him changed and scarred. Throughout, the musical emphasizes the cost of war, the trials of heroism, and the enduring power of home, family, and love.

Here's a short timeline for the story of the movie!

Trojan War Ends

After ten long years of war, Odysseus devises the Trojan Horse, leading to Troy's fall and the Greeks' victory.

Encounter with Polyphemus

Odysseus blinds the Cyclops Polyphemus, earning the deadly curse of Poseidon.

Sirens, Scylla, and Charybdis

He survives deadly sea monsters and the Sirens' song through wit and sacrifice.

Island of the Sun God

His crew disobeys the gods by killing Helios' sacred cattle, sealing their fate.

Calypso's Island

Odysseus is trapped for years until the gods command Calypso to release him.

Return to Ithaca

Disguised as a beggar, Odysseus defeats the suitors and reunites with Penelope.
